Returns to Education

Whilst at the Department for Education (DfE), I worked extensively on the returns to school-age qualifications. This primarily used the Longitudinal Education Outcomes (LEO) dataset, which links administrative data held by DfE, DWP, and HMRC. Published work includes estimates of the associations between GCSE attainment and lifetime earnings, and KS2 attainment and early labour market outcomes.

These estimates are used internally at DfE to support economic appraisal of programmes and policies. To aid this, I wrote the schools policy appraisal handbook, an unofficial Green Book supplement and the first DfE guidance on how to use returns estimates in appraisal.

As well as producing a range of economic appraisals internally at DfE, I published two worked examples of using returns estimates to calculate the economic benefits of effective reception classes and meeting the ambitions set out in the 2022 Schools White Paper.
